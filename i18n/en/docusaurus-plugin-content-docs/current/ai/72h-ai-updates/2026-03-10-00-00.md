## 🔥 Spotlight: The Anthropic-U.S. Government "Breakup"

If we were to select the most dramatic event of the week, the public conflict between Anthropic and the U.S. Department of Defense would undoubtedly take the crown. This controversy has not only gripped Silicon Valley but has also sparked intense global discussions about the military applications of AI.

### How It Unfolded: From Cooperation to Confrontation

The story began when Anthropic's AI model, Claude, was used in U.S. military operations against Iran. According to The Washington Post, the U.S. military leveraged AI technology to strike 1,000 targets in the first 24 hours of its attack on Iran, with Claude serving as a core tool in this operation. However, when Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei learned of this usage, he made a decision that enraged the Pentagon — publicly opposing the use of Claude for such military purposes.

Amodei's concerns centered on two main issues: first, the potential for AI to be used for mass domestic surveillance, and second, the ethical risks associated with autonomous military targeting. These concerns were not unfounded. In meetings with U.S. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth, Amodei explicitly expressed Anthropic's reservations about these applications.

The Pentagon's response was equally forceful. Under Secretary of Defense Emil Michael stated publicly on X: "I want to end all speculation: there is no active [Department of War] negotiation with [Anthropic]." Even more severe was the Pentagon's decision to label Anthropic as a "supply chain risk" enterprise — the first time an American company has received such a designation. This means the U.S. government considers Anthropic not secure enough for its use.

### Reactions from All Sides: An International Chess Game

The impact of this conflict quickly spread globally. London Mayor Sadiq Khan astutely seized the opportunity to extend an "invitation" to Anthropic. In a public letter, he wrote: "I applaud your steadfastness in the face of such pressure... it would be good to discuss how we could support you to expand operations further." Khan even stated that London "could provide an even more significant location and platform for the future of Anthropic."

The White House's response was filled with hostility. When asked about the mayor's invitation, a White House spokesperson told the BBC: "As President Trump said, we will never allow a radical left, woke company to dictate how our United States Military fights wars."

Microsoft's stance appeared more pragmatic. Despite the Pentagon's warning to Anthropic, Microsoft stated it would continue integrating Anthropic's technology into its products, with only an exception clause for government clients. This "don't offend either side" strategy perhaps represents the true choice most tech companies make when facing political pressure.

### Deeper Reflections: The Ethical Dilemma of AI Militarization

This conflict reveals a deeper question: When AI technology is used for military purposes, who decides where the boundaries lie?

Historically, cooperation between technology companies and the military is not uncommon. From the Manhattan Project to the origins of the internet, many world-changing technologies have emerged from military needs. But AI is different because its application boundaries are extremely blurred. An AI system used to analyze satellite imagery can be used to monitor crop growth or identify military targets; an algorithm used to optimize logistics can improve commercial efficiency or optimize weapon delivery.

Anthropic's stance represents a "principles first" value system. Amodei was willing to risk losing government contracts and being labeled a "supply chain risk" to maintain his ethical bottom line. This kind of courage is rare in Silicon Valley. As AI critic Gary Marcus stated: "Questionable character in a man with this much power to affect the world is dangerous."

However, the Pentagon's position also has its validity. In modern warfare, technological advantage often means the difference between life and death. If the United States abandons the use of AI due to ethical concerns while adversaries have no such reservations, the consequences could be disastrous. This "prisoner's dilemma" logic is precisely why AI militarization is so difficult to stop.

### My Perspective

Honestly, I feel both concerned and relieved about this conflict. Concerned because the train of AI militarization seems to have already left the station and is difficult to stop. Whether or not Anthropic participates, other companies — perhaps those with fewer ethical scruples — will fill the void. Relieved because at least there are people like Amodei willing to stand up and say no.

I believe the key to solving this problem lies not in preventing AI's military applications — which is practically impossible — but in establishing clear rules and transparent oversight mechanisms. If we cannot prevent AI from entering the battlefield, at least we can ensure its use is subject to democratic processes rather than being decided solely by a company's CEO or a general.

## 🚀 AI Product Updates: From Lab to Battlefield

### U.S. Military AI Applications Spark Debate

One of the most shocking news items from the past 72 hours is the actual application of AI in the Iran conflict. According to MIT Technology Review, the U.S. military used multiple AI tools, including Claude, when striking Iranian targets. Surprisingly, these tools were not only used for target identification but also for real-time intelligence analysis and decision support.

An online intelligence dashboard developed by two employees at Andreessen Horowitz gained popularity during the conflict. This dashboard combines satellite imagery, ship tracking data, news feeds, and prediction markets, allowing users to track the progress of military operations in real-time. What's even more surprising is that most of these dashboards were "vibe-coded" products — developers used AI tools to complete development within days.

This phenomenon raises an interesting question: When anyone can quickly develop complex intelligence analysis systems using AI tools, are traditional information control mechanisms still effective? Planet Labs, a satellite imagery company, stopped sharing relevant images after discovering they were being used to track Iranian attacks. But can this "after-the-fact remedy" truly prevent information dissemination? I have my doubts.

### NVIDIA Releases "State of AI Report 2026"

Beyond the controversies, NVIDIA's "State of AI Report 2026" provides us with a more macroscopic industry perspective. Based on responses from over 3,200 enterprises, this report reveals several key trends:

**Enterprise AI Adoption is Maturing**: More and more companies are moving from pilot phases to scaled deployment. Unlike the "testing the waters" mentality of previous years, companies now focus on how to truly integrate AI into core business processes.

**Open Source Tools Drive AI Strategy**: The report notes that companies increasingly prefer using open-source tools to build specialized AI programs to address specific challenges. This contrasts sharply with the "one-size-fits-all" approach of closed-source models.

**The Rise of Agentic AI**: The report specifically highlights the rise of "Agentic AI" — AI systems capable of autonomous task execution that are moving from concept to practical application.

**ROI Becomes Apparent**: The most encouraging news for enterprise decision-makers is that AI is helping increase annual revenue, reduce costs, and boost productivity. This means AI is no longer just a "money-burning" technology experiment but a tool that can truly create value.

However, the report also points out a key challenge: the shortage of AI experts. Despite AI tools becoming increasingly user-friendly, talent capable of strategically deploying these tools remains scarce. This perhaps explains why AI engineer salaries continue to rise.

If Anthropic is holding firm to its principles under external pressure, then OpenAI is struggling with an internal trust crisis. AI critic Gary Marcus, in an article titled "Sam Altman's Greed and Dishonesty Are Finally Catching Up to Him," detailed a series of recent controversies surrounding OpenAI founder and CEO Sam Altman.

Marcus points out that Altman's "two-faced" behavior — publicly supporting competitor Anthropic's Dario Amodei while simultaneously negotiating behind his back and supporting surveillance measures — has become "the last straw." Millions of users feel angry and betrayed; "nobody wishes to be surveilled."

Even more embarrassing for OpenAI was the resignation of Caitlin Kalinowski, head of the company's robotics division. Her resignation statement hinted that her departure was related to concerns about Altman's personal character. This is not the first time OpenAI has attracted attention due to high-level departures. Since the board briefly fired Altman in November 2023, OpenAI's core team has lost several key figures.

While Marcus's commentary carries obvious personal bias, it does touch on a core issue: When a company's founder is questioned for being "not consistently candid," will the company's long-term development be affected? For OpenAI, which is seeking a new round of funding that could value the company at hundreds of billions of dollars, this question is particularly important.

### UK AI Investments Exposed as "Phantom Investments"

An investigation by The Guardian has uncovered serious problems in the UK government's AI investment plans. Since 2024, the UK government has announced a series of massive AI investment agreements involving data center construction, supercomputers, and thousands of jobs. However, the investigation found that many of these investments may be nothing more than "phantoms."

Take NScale as an example. The company recently announced it had completed a $2 billion funding round, with its valuation soaring to $14.6 billion. Former UK Deputy Prime Minister Nick Clegg and former Meta COO Sheryl Sandberg also joined the board. But The Guardian found that the claimed £1.9 billion investment had no formal contract to support it; the promised data centers might be old facilities rather than new constructions; the jobs were unaccounted for; and the supercomputer site located 12 miles north of London remains a scaffolding storage yard.

The UK Department for Science, Innovation and Technology refused to answer detailed questions but stated it "rejected these assertions," claiming that the UK AI sector has attracted over £100 billion in private investment since the government took office. However, the department also admitted it is "not playing an active role in auditing these commitments."

This incident raises questions about the global AI investment boom. With over £500 billion promised in AI investments globally in 2025, how much of it is real? In an industry filled with hype and bubbles, how do investors and policymakers distinguish between genuine innovation and empty promises?

### ABB Robotics Partners with NVIDIA to Advance Industrial-Grade Physical AI

Beyond the controversies, there are also genuine technological advances being made through collaboration. ABB Robotics and NVIDIA announced a partnership to leverage the NVIDIA Omniverse platform to deliver industrial-grade physical AI at scale.

The core of this collaboration is combining NVIDIA's simulation technology with ABB's industrial robots, enabling robots to learn and test in virtual environments before being deployed to actual production lines. This approach can significantly reduce the cost and time of robot programming while improving safety and reliability.

For the manufacturing industry, this could be a game-changer. Traditional industrial robots require extensive manual programming, and each production line adjustment requires reconfiguration. AI-based robots can autonomously learn new tasks through simulation, making automation more feasible for small and medium-sized enterprises.

## 💡 Open Source Highlights

### LLMs-from-scratch: Building ChatGPT from Scratch

One of the hottest AI projects on GitHub this week is rasbt's LLMs-from-scratch. This project provides a complete tutorial for implementing a ChatGPT-like large language model from scratch in PyTorch, having already received over 87,000 stars.

The project's value lies in demystifying large language models. For many developers, LLMs are like black boxes — input text, output results, with no understanding of what happens in between. This project uncovers the core concepts of attention mechanisms, Transformer architecture, and training techniques step by step, allowing anyone to understand and implement their own LLM.

This educational significance extends far beyond the technology itself. In an era where AI capabilities are becoming increasingly concentrated, open-source educational projects are helping to distribute this power. If more people can understand and build their own AI systems, dependence on a few tech giants will decrease, and the resilience of the entire industry will increase.

### Cherry Studio: Open Source AI Productivity Suite

Another noteworthy open-source project is Cherry Studio, an AI productivity studio integrating smart chat, autonomous agents, and over 300 assistants. It provides a unified access interface to frontier LLMs, allowing users to use multiple different AI models within a single interface.

The rise of such projects reflects a trend: users do not want to be locked into a single AI provider's ecosystem. They want the flexibility to switch between different models, choosing the most suitable tool for each task. Open-source solutions perfectly meet this need.

### "AI Brain Fry": The Cognitive Limits of Monitoring AI Agents

The Decoder reported on an interesting study: when workers supervise AI agents for extended periods, they experience what is called "AI Brain Fry." The study found that humans have a cognitive load limit when monitoring AI systems; beyond this limit, human judgment and reaction speed significantly decline.

This discovery serves as an important warning for enterprises deploying AI agents at scale. Simply putting AI systems into operation is not enough; consideration must also be given to how to design human-machine collaboration interfaces to ensure human supervisors can maintain effective working conditions.

### Millions Already Using AI Chatbots for Financial Advice

Another study shows that millions of users are already using AI chatbots like ChatGPT for financial advice. Although experts warn that these tools have clear limitations — they may provide outdated or inaccurate information and cannot understand an individual's complete financial situation — users don't seem to mind.

This phenomenon reflects a pain point in the financial services industry: professional financial advisors are too expensive for ordinary people, while free online information is too chaotic. AI chatbots perfectly fill this gap, providing a "good enough" middle-ground solution. Of course, this also brings risks: if AI gives wrong advice, who bears the responsibility?

## 🤔 Reflections: AI at a Crossroads

Reviewing the AI developments of the past 72 hours, I feel a strong sense of contradiction.

On one hand, AI technology is advancing at an astonishing pace. From military applications to industrial automation, from open-source education to personal productivity tools, AI is penetrating every corner of society. NVIDIA's report shows that enterprises are moving from experimental phases to scaled deployment, and AI is creating real economic value.

On the other hand, this industry is also filled with controversy and uncertainty. The Anthropic-Pentagon conflict, the OpenAI trust crisis, the exposure of UK "phantom investments" — these events remind us that AI's development is not smooth sailing. Technological capabilities are outpacing our ethical frameworks and governance mechanisms.

I believe we are at a critical crossroads in AI development. In the coming years, we will decide whether AI becomes a tool that benefits humanity or a weapon that exacerbates social division and inequality. This choice will not happen automatically; it requires the active participation of every practitioner, policymaker, and citizen.

As AI practitioners, we have a responsibility to ensure that technology's direction of development aligns with humanity's long-term interests. This is not just a technical issue but a values issue. When profit conflicts with ethics, what should we choose? When efficiency and fairness cannot coexist, how should we balance them?

These questions have no simple answers. But as Anthropic's Amodei has shown, at least we should have the courage to ask these questions and say "no" when necessary.
